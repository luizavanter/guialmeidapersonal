# The Importance of Nutrition in Training

<div class="blog-meta">
  <span>By Guilherme Almeida</span> • <span>December 2025</span> • <span class="reading-time">6 min read</span>
</div>


**"Abs are made in the kitchen."** You've heard that, right? But the truth goes further: all your results — weight loss, muscle gain, performance — are made in the kitchen. Training is just the stimulus.

## The Real Fitness Equation

```
Results = Training (30%) + Nutrition (50%) + Recovery (20%)
```

You can train perfectly, but if your nutrition is poor, you're sabotaging 50% of your results.

## Why Is Nutrition So Important?

### 1. Training Doesn't Build Muscle

Shocking, but true. Training **destroys** muscle fibers. Building happens afterward, during recovery, and DEPENDS on adequate nutrients.

**Without proper nutrition:**
- Muscles don't fully recover
- You don't get stronger
- Risk of overtraining increases
- Immune system weakens

### 2. You Can't Out-Train a Bad Diet

"I'll eat pizza today but train hard tomorrow to make up for it."

**Brutal math:**
- 1 pizza: ~2,000 calories
- 1 hour of intense training: ~400-600 calories burned

You'd need to train 3-4 hours the next day. Impossible and counterproductive.

### 3. Performance Depends on Fuel

You're only as strong as your fuel allows. Training without eating properly is like driving a sports car on low-grade gasoline.

## The 3 Pillars of Nutrition for Results

### Pillar 1: Protein — The Builder

**Why it's essential:**
- Repairs and builds muscle fibers
- Increases satiety (hunger control)
- Keeps metabolism elevated
- Preserves lean mass during caloric deficit

**How much you need:**
- Weight loss: 2-2.2g per kg
- Maintenance: 1.6-1.8g per kg
- Muscle gain: 2-2.5g per kg

**Best sources:**
- Chicken, fish, lean meat
- Whole eggs
- Whey protein
- Dairy (Greek yogurt, cottage cheese)
- Legumes (for vegans)

### Pillar 2: Carbohydrates — The Fuel

**Common mistake:** Cutting carbs completely.

**Truth:** Carbs are not the enemy. They're essential for:
- Energy during intense workouts
- Muscle recovery (glycogen replenishment)
- Hormone production
- Mental performance

**Timing is everything:**
- Pre-workout (1-2h before): Complex carbs
- Post-workout (up to 2h after): Simple carbs + protein
- Other meals: Complex carbs in moderation

**Best sources:**
- Sweet potato, brown rice, oatmeal
- Fruits (banana pre-workout is a classic)
- Whole grain bread, whole wheat pasta
- Quinoa, potato

### Pillar 3: Fats — The Regulator

**Why you need them:**
- Hormone production (testosterone, estrogen)
- Absorption of vitamins A, D, E, K
- Prolonged satiety
- Joint and cognitive health

**How much:** 20-30% of total calories

**Best sources:**
- Olive oil, avocado
- Nuts, almonds
- Salmon, sardines
- Whole eggs
- Natural peanut butter

## Timing: When You Eat Matters

### Pre-Workout (1-2h before)

**Goal:** Energy for intense training

**Ideal:**
- Complex carbs + moderate protein
- Example: Sweet potato + chicken breast
- Example 2: Oatmeal + whey protein

### Post-Workout (Anabolic Window: up to 2h)

**Goal:** Recovery and muscle building

**Ideal:**
- Simple carbs + fast protein
- Example: Banana + whey protein
- Example 2: White rice + chicken

### Before Bed

**Goal:** Keep the body in an anabolic state during sleep

**Ideal:**
- Slow-digesting protein
- Example: Greek yogurt or casein

## Hydration: The Forgotten Nutrient

Just 2% dehydration reduces performance by up to 20%.

**Daily goal:**
- 35ml per kg of body weight
- Example: 80kg = 2.8L of water per day
- Add 500-1,000ml per hour of training

**Signs of dehydration:**
- Dark yellow urine
- Excessive fatigue
- Headaches
- Muscle cramps

## Practical Planning: Sample Day

**Target: 80kg, muscle gain goal**

**Breakfast (7 AM):**
- 3 scrambled eggs
- 2 slices whole grain bread
- 1 banana
- Coffee
- Total: ~500 calories, 35g protein

**Snack (10 AM):**
- Greek yogurt
- 1 serving oatmeal
- Total: ~250 calories, 20g protein

**Lunch (1 PM):**
- 150g grilled chicken
- 150g brown rice
- Full salad
- Total: ~600 calories, 45g protein

**Pre-Workout (4 PM):**
- Medium sweet potato
- Whey protein
- Total: ~300 calories, 30g protein

**Post-Workout (6:30 PM):**
- Banana
- Whey protein
- Total: ~200 calories, 30g protein

**Dinner (8 PM):**
- 150g salmon
- Assorted vegetables
- Olive oil
- Total: ~500 calories, 40g protein

**Before Bed (10 PM):**
- Cottage cheese or casein
- Total: ~150 calories, 25g protein

**Daily Total:** ~2,500 calories, 225g protein

## Nutrition and Training Myths

### Myth 1: "I need to eat every 3 hours"

**Truth:** Meal frequency matters less than daily totals. 3-5 meals work equally well.

### Myth 2: "Carbs at night turn into fat"

**Truth:** Total daily caloric intake is what matters, not the specific time.

### Myth 3: "I need expensive shakes and supplements"

**Truth:** Real food is always the priority. Supplements are convenience, not necessity.

### Myth 4: "My friend's diet will work for me"

**Truth:** Individualization is key. Metabolism, genetics, routine — everything varies.

## Signs Your Nutrition Is Poor

- ❌ No energy to train
- ❌ Slow recovery between workouts
- ❌ Not gaining strength/muscle despite training well
- ❌ Constant or extreme hunger
- ❌ Poor sleep
- ❌ Low immunity (getting sick frequently)

If you checked 2+ signs, your nutrition needs urgent adjustments.

## Conclusion

Training without proper nutrition is like building a house without cement. You can have the best bricks (exercises), but nothing will hold together.

The good news? Nutrition doesn't have to be complicated. Adequate protein, carbs at the right time, quality fats, and hydration. Simple as that.
